Product Philosophy Manifesto

Trace isn’t about features. It’s about obviousness. It behaves the way your mind expects—even before you realize what you want.

1. Expectation is Specification

We don’t build features. We fulfill reflexes. If a user tries something, Trace should already support it. Esc to dismiss, arrow keys to navigate. It just works.

2. Interfaces Should Be Invisible

UI is not the product—reaction is. Trace feels like muscle memory, not software. Minimal surface, maximal reflex.

3. Everything Is Intent

Typing is search. Selection is decision. Escape is exit. Trace responds to your behavior, not just your clicks.

4. Behavior is the Documentation

If it needs to be explained, it’s wrong. Users shouldn’t learn Trace—they should recognize it. Zero cognitive overhead.

Transparent by Design

Your trust is our most important asset. Here is our simple data promise.

1. Your Content is Sacred

Trace operates 100% locally today. **We will never collect, read, or transmit the content of your clips and stashes.** That is your private data, period.

2. Built for Improvement

To make Trace better, we plan to collect anonymous usage data in the future (e.g., how often a feature is used). This data will never contain your personal content.

3. You Are in Control

When we do introduce analytics, **you will have the clear right to opt-out.** Your privacy choices will always be respected.
